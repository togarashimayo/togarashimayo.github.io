Duck dinner (Duck Hunt inspired)

Duck sauce recipe adapted from Always Use Butter

Ingredients

  • 3 pieces of duck leg, duck breast, or both (I used two legs and a breast)
  • 454 grams broccoli
  • 908 grams potatoes
  • 250 mL orange juice
  • 4 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 5 mL thyme
  • 60 mL honey

Steps

Ducks

  1. Pat dry the parts, score diagonal hatches in them, and season with salt and pepper. Do not score them deep enough that you can see the dark red flesh. This “renders” the duck.
  2. Place the duck leg(s) ungreased and bake for 425 degrees for 90-120 minutes.
  3. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat without any sort of fat. Add the duck breasts skin-side-down and sear for 5 minutes.
  4. Flip the breasts and sear for another 5 minutes.
  5. Place breast(s) skin-side-up on another baking sheet and bake in the oven (also at 425 degrees) for 10-20 minutes.
  6. Slice duck into bite sized strips, drizzle with sauce, and enjoy!

Duck sauce

  1. Add 30 mL of the reserved duck fat into a small saucepan and heat over medium-high heat.
  2. Add orange juice, honey, thyme, and garlic cloves to the duck fat. Cook until the sauce is reduced by half (around 5-10 minutes)
  3. Turn off the heat and remove the garlic cloves (…or keep them in) from the sauce.

Duck veggies

  1. Toss duck fat in the vegetables with salt and pepper to taste, and tray all that on a parchment-lined pan.
  2. Bake for 15-20 minutes at 425 degrees.
