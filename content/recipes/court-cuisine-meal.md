# Court cuisine full course meal

## Pumpkin soup

I used [The Salty Marshmallow’s recipe](https://thesaltymarshmallow.com/easy-pumpkin-soup/), but omitted the ginger and caramelized the onions for 45 minutes.

## Pan-fried fish

### Ingredients

- Five filets of your favourite firm white fish
- 15 mL neutral oil
- At least 30 mL flour
- 1.25 mL salt
- few dashes pepper

### Steps

1. Pat the filets dry and cut into McNugget sized pieces.
2. Lightly dredge the pieces in flour seasoned with salt and pepper.
3. In a non-stick pan, heat oil over medium heat. Once hot, cook the salmon for 3 minutes on each side.
4. Transfer the fish to a plate.

## Ducks

1. Pat dry the parts, score diagonal hatches in them, and season with salt and pepper. Do not score them deep enough that you can see the dark red flesh.
2. Place the duck leg(s) ungreased and bake for 425 degrees for 90-120 minutes.
3. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat without any sort of fat. Add the duck breasts skin-side-down and sear for 5 minutes.
4. Flip the breasts and sear for another 5 minutes.
5. Place breast(s) skin-side-up on another baking sheet and bake in the oven (also at 425 degrees) for 10-20 minutes.
6. Slice duck into bite sized strips, drizzle with red wine sauce, and enjoy!

## Duck sauce

### Ingredients

- 1 shallot
- 22.5 mL flour
- 250 mL chicken stock OR enough dry bouillon for 250 mL of chicken stock
- 250 mL red wine
- 5 mL lemon juice or apple cider vinegar
- 45 mL butter or duck fat

### Steps

1. Finely dice the shallot.
2. After searing your duck breasts, remove all but 15 mL of the duck fat.
3. Stir-fry the shallot for a minute in the hot oil.
4. Add the flour and combine to make a roux.
5. Gradually add the chicken stock and the red wine.
6. Cook on high (stirring occasionally) until the liquid coats the back of a spoon. 
7. Mix butter or duck fat into the sauce and serve immediately.

## Notes

- Duck breasts and legs are available at your local butcher shop (I got mine from Windsor *Quality* Meats, but I’ve seen Beefway sell them). They are very likely to come frozen as they are a niche meat.
- If you want a wedge cheese for decoration, Whole Foods sells 50% off some cheese.
- Any round loaf bought from a bakery and grocery store works (I used Breka’s San Francisco sourdough)
