# Grilled "kelpie"

**Warning: Do not try to replicate the OG plating by putting raw meat and vegetables on the same plate (or very close to each other); this will very likely result in the meat's bacteria transferring to the vegetables.**

*Oxtail recipe adapted from [Dads Cook Dinner’s Instant Pot Oxtail recipe](https://www.dadcooksdinner.com/instant-pot-easy-braised-oxtail/)*

## Grilled meat and veggies

### Ingredients

- 227g of your favourite non-liver beef cuts (per person)
- 1/2 lb of beef liver (optional)
- 1 small head cabbage
- 1 medium onion

### Steps

1. Thinly cut the onion into rings
2. Halve, core, and quarter the cabbage.
3. Cut your meat into bite-sized pieces and strips
4. Heat your grill according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
5. Veggies: Cook the veggies until they are nicely browned on the bottom, before flipping them over to brown on the other side.
6. Non-liver meat: Cook for 1-2 minutes per side.
7. Liver: Cook for 2-3 minutes per side.

## Kelpie "mane" salad

I used [Serious Eats's seaweed salad recipe](https://www.seriouseats.com/seaweed-salad-recipe) without making any modifications.

## Braised oxtail

### Ingredients

- 7.5 mL neutral oil
- ~680g oxtails
- 2.5 mL salt
- 1.25 mL freshly ground black pepper
- 125 mL water + enough powdered bouillon for 250 mL worth of broth
- 1.25 mL dried thyme
- 7.5 mL Worcestershire sauce
- 7.5 mL soy sauce

### Steps

1. Remove the oxtail blood by soaking the oxtail in cool water for 1 hour. Your oxtail should be pale with the water being a pale red from the blood.
2. Rinse the oxtail, pat dry, and season with salt and pepper.
3. Heat oil in a small saucepan. When hot, sear the oxtail on each side for 5 minutes.
4. Add the water and scrape the bottom of the pot to loosen any browned bits stuck to the bottom.
5. Add the bouillon, thyme, Worcestershire sauce, and soy sauce. 
6. Bring the liquid to a boil. Once boiled, turn the heat to low and cook for three hours.
7. Carefully remove the oxtails from the saucepan using tongs or a slotted spoon, separate the fat to use the liquid as a sauce, and enjoy!

# Notes

- If you have any dog-owning friends, you can feed them any leftover grilled liver.
- One of my family members found it tasty adding the braising liquid to the rice. They likes how the oil coats the rice well.
- Beef is expensive. I recommend taking advantage of clearance sales (I used Safeway’s Enjoy Tonight as a Canadian) and special deals.
- Senshi didn't make any sauces for the kelpie, but I made [this sauce](https://www.thespruceeats.com/yakiniku-sauce-for-grilled-meat-2030920) (while using garlic powder instead of garlic and omitting the sesame seeds) for a family member.
